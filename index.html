Frequently asked

Questions worth asking before you build.

Good projects begin with honest conversations. These are some of the topics we address early.

Ask a different question ↗
When should we involve a builder?

Earlier than most homeowners expect. Builder input during planning can help align scope, materials, access, sequencing and investment expectations before drawings or selections become difficult to change.

Do you work with architects and designers?

Yes. The site is structured to position the company as a collaborative construction partner, whether the client already has a design team or needs introductions and coordination support.

How are project changes handled?

Changes should be documented with clear impact to scope, cost and timing before the related work moves forward. Replace this language with the company’s exact change-order policy.

Can clients remain in the home during renovation?

Sometimes. It depends on project scope, utilities, access and safety. The planning phase should establish realistic expectations around occupied zones, temporary protection and potential relocation.

What areas do you serve?

The current template is positioned for Nassau and Suffolk County, including the North Shore, South Shore and East End. Update the exact service territory before launch.
